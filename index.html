Bio

I am currently at the Federal Reserve supporting senior leadership in supervising the largest, most systemically important financial institutions that operate within the United States. Prior to this, I was a member of the Large Bank Analytics and Supervisory Research and Analysis Units of the Federal Reserve Bank of Boston (FRBB) where I regularly briefed President Eric Rosengren and other senior members of the FRBB on key trends and developments within the banking industry.

Prior to joining the FRBB, I was a Senior Global Financials Analyst at Calvert Investments, one of the oldest socially responsible investors within the United States, managing the Financials sleeve of the four active equity portfolios (AUM: $2.5 billion) and helping develop an ESG investment framework for financial services companies. I also previously held roles at UBS supporting industry leading senior analysts in covering 39 financial services companies with a combined market capitalization of over $1.7 trillion and Ernst & Young in New York City managing a team of consultants in implementing ERM systems for clients and assisting clients within the Financials space on international and state & local tax planning and compliance issues.

I recieved an A.L.M. in Extension Studies, Information Management Systems from Harvard University with a focus on cybersecurity. Additionally I recieved a B.S. in accounting and a JD (admitted to the NJ and NY Bars) from the Rutgers School of Business and Rutgers Law School, respectively. I also possesses the CPA and CFA designations.
